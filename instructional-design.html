Instructional Design

Teaching Philosophy
& Design Thinking

"Good learning design is empathic, user-centered, and uncomplicated in its path to learning."

Philosophy

Core Belief
Learning happens by doing, not by receiving.
Whether it's a K-12 student building a project or an employee applying a new workflow, the goal is always transfer — can they actually use this in the real world? The measure of good design is not what was taught but what changed.
Accessibility First
Design for the most marginalized learner first.
With deep experience in Title-1 districts and ESL contexts, accessibility is not an afterthought — it is the starting point. When you design for the learner with the most barriers, you design better for everyone. Differentiated instruction is not accommodation; it is good design.
K–12 Approach
Project-based learning, rooted in real context.
Students learn best when the work is meaningful and the stakes are real. Projects that connect to students' lives, communities, and futures create the conditions for genuine understanding — not just performance on an assessment.
Corporate Approach
Performance-based design, working backwards from outcomes.
In corporate contexts, every learning experience starts with a business outcome: what does someone need to do differently? Working backwards from that question ensures training is targeted, efficient, and measurable — reducing time-to-competency and maximizing ROI.

Design Models & Frameworks

ADDIE
Analyze · Design · Develop · Implement · Evaluate
The foundational ID framework. Used for structured course development across both K-12 and corporate contexts, ensuring each phase informs the next.
SAM
Successive Approximation Model
An agile alternative to ADDIE. Preferred for projects with evolving requirements or tight timelines — rapid prototyping with continuous feedback loops.
Bloom's
Bloom's Taxonomy
Used to write precise learning objectives and design assessments that match the intended cognitive level — from recall to creation.
UDL
Universal Design for Learning
Guides the design of flexible learning experiences with multiple means of representation, engagement, and expression — essential for diverse and ESL learner populations.
TPACK
Technological Pedagogical Content Knowledge
The framework for integrating technology thoughtfully — ensuring tools serve the pedagogy, not the other way around.
Kirkpatrick
Kirkpatrick Model of Evaluation
Used to evaluate training effectiveness at four levels: reaction, learning, behavior, and results. Essential for demonstrating ROI to corporate stakeholders.
