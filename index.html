Layered Identities

An interactive map of Portugal surfacing the lived experiences of immigrant communities through contemporary traditional dress. Instructional Design K–12 Education AI Literacy Curriculum

Teaching with AI

A Micro-Course for K–12 Educators

Sample

Three-module micro-course (4–6 hours, 0.5 CEU) asking: what does it mean to teach well in an age of AI? Built on constructivist and critical pedagogy foundations, each module follows an orient → explore → apply → reflect arc and ends with a practical classroom deliverable.

01

AI Tool Literacy

A functional, honest mental model of how AI works — and the tools already in students' hands. Culminates in a personal AI Landscape Map.

3 lessons · ~90 min · Video + Guided Exploration
02

AI Ethics in Education

Frameworks for academic integrity, algorithmic bias, and student data privacy. Culminates in a draft Classroom AI Policy.

3 lessons · ~100 min · Case Studies + Scenario Practice
03

Pedagogy First

The HEART framework for AI-resilient assignment design. Positions AI as a thinking partner, not a shortcut. Culminates in one redesigned lesson.

3 lessons · ~110 min · Design Studio + Peer Review
Constructivism Critical Pedagogy UDL TPACK Algorithmic Justice

About

Work that lives at the edge of systems and story — where learning design meets cultural inquiry, and interfaces carry meaning.

Hana Morsi is a designer and researcher working across instructional design, interactive media, and cultural narrative. Under the studio name hyasmo, she builds projects that resist easy categorization — tools that teach, installations that ask questions, and interfaces that feel like writing.

Current focus: participatory systems, immigrant visibility, and the poetics of UX.
